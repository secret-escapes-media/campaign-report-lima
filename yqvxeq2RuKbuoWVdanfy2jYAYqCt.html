    2016

    Campaign Overview

    Phase 2 focused on a mainstream audience by positioning Peru as an accessible destination; a place where you can eat well, immerse yourself in the diverse culture, and explore the fascinating topography. We wanted to make consumers aware of a new, direct route to the capital Lima with British Airways.

    Part of the campaign consisted of pulling together local experts to get their recommendations and top, insider tips on Peru, in conjunction with beautiful 360° imagery.

    1. Nine themed pages, featuring curated and diverse content on Peru
    2. Eight 360° images
    3. Five experts (advocates) from the world of photography, gastronomy, and social media to help create credible and exclusive content
    4. One social media Instagram takeover featuring landscape and adventure photographer Jacob Moon, who has 356k Instagram followers

    Highlights

    1. Email performed very well, with the open and click through rate surpassing the average benchmark: 28.03% OR & 1.66% CTR
    2. Competition page generated 13,212 entries. This is 12 times higher than the Lima Phase 1 campaign which received 7,444 visits & 1,256 entries over six weeks
    3. Strong social media presence across the two weeks – entire activity had over one million impressions. Highest two posts received a reach of 371,765 & 181,432
    4. The site received 58,383 unique views
    5. We sent 2,225 clicks from the Hub through to British Airways flights booking page
    6. All nine Instagram takeover posts became our best performing posts in the week of the 31st October
    7. 360° images received 13,536 clicks in total. Paraglide Miraflores, on the overview and adventure page, received the most clicks at 7,857

    Learnings

    1. The Hub generated over 58k unique views – this is under our target of 65k but still a firm result for a two week campaign. This was achieved by high social media activity and various platforms driving to the Hub page. For future campaigns the duration should be longer than two weeks; this will enable more time for our optimizations to produce higher results
    2. Given the focus on driving traffic to the Hub over such a short time period, the CTR was slightly diluted, however refreshed display creatives helped here
    3. Spacing out the posts would have enabled even higher interactions on social media and maximised engagement
    4. Having tested different subject lines, the second was the best performing as it mentioned the competition. It delivered 47.84% OR and 5.45% CTR, more than double the benchmarks
    5. Both HPTOs generated an above average 2.55% CTR. The second HPTO skin performed the best as the tagline highlighted the competition element further, achieving 6,319 clicks
    6. We tested different titles for the top navigation and display taglines. Again, we found mention of the competition performed the best
